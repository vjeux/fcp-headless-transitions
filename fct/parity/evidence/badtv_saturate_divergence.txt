## UPDATE 2026-07-23t — desaturate leg partially decoded; confirmed entangled (needs isolated rig)
After completing the colour subsystem (all transfers VERIFIED), revisited BadTV's desaturate leg
with the scanF-normalization trick: gray 128 -> 163.1 (constant for Saturate<=0) gives a per-patch
scanline-average factor scanF=1.274 (the patch spans dark+bright scan bands, so this is the MEAN
band factor, not a pointwise lift). Dividing every colour output by scanF backs out the pre-scanline
desaturate result:
  green (50,200,50) @Saturate=0 -> premix (129.9, 190.5, 116.9)
Solving out_c = L + (in_c - L)*sat on the R,G channels gives sat=0.404, L=184.1 — i.e. Saturate=0
is NOT identity (sat=1); FCP's Saturate->sat CPU map is NOT v/100+1 (that gives 1.0). BUT the model
BREAKS on the B channel: R and B share input 50 yet map to 129.9 vs 116.9 — equal inputs, unequal
outputs. So a scalar mix(luma,rgb,sat) cannot be the whole story: there is an ADDITIONAL per-channel
term (chromatic-aberration P11 splits R/G/B sampling even at Waviness=0, and/or the scanF is not
perfectly uniform across the R/B sample positions). CONCLUSION: BadTV's "pointwise" desaturate is
irreducibly entangled with chromatic aberration + scanline banding in the current uniform-patch
probe. To decode cleanly needs an ISOLATED rig: P11 aberration=0, single pinned scanline phase (read
ONE scanline row, not a patch average), then solve sat(Saturate) + luma coeffs P12 from equal-input
colours. Until that rig exists BadTV stays DIVERGED (delegated faithful, ddb=11.3). The scanF=1.274
mean-band factor and sat≈0.4-at-Saturate=0 are concrete, reproducible leads for that rig.

## RESOLVED (desaturate leg) 2026-07-23u — ISOLATED RIG kills the scanline confound; Rec.709 exact
The entire earlier "confounded/entangled" conclusion was a PROBE ARTIFACT: the default Scan Line
Brightness=1.5 makes the shader's scanF = mix(darkFloor=1.5, 1, smoothstep) vary across the patch,
so a uniform-patch center-read averaged dark+bright bands into a fake ~1.27x lift AND a fake
"desaturation at Saturate=0". Setting Scan Line Brightness=1 forces scanF=1 EVERYWHERE (mix(1,1,·)=1),
giving a CLEAN pointwise desaturate:
  - gray 128 -> 128 (no lift), Saturate=0 -> identity (all colours unchanged).
  - Saturate=-100 (sat=0) -> pure luma gray; coeffs = Rec.709 (0.2124,0.7138,0.0735), sum 1.0.
  - mix(luma709, rgb, Saturate/100+1) in CODE space matches the DESATURATION leg (Saturate<=0) at
    worst 0.4 lvl (7 colours × 3 levels). Old fitted coeffs 0.2581/0.5856/0.1611 were 18.9 lvl off.
FIXED in engine badtv.ts (commit dbb38d2). Gate-relevant: default Saturate=-25 is a desaturation.
The OVER-saturation leg (Saturate>0) diverges in code space (worst 105) — pushes chroma out of
gamut; needs the gamma-1.958 WS + gamut treatment (WS alone got it to 84, not closed) — but no
built-in host oversaturates. The delegated filter.PAEBadTV stays DIVERGED: its whole-frame
delta-response is dominated by frame-peak AGC + scanline banding + Static noise, NOT the (now-fixed)
desaturate. Durable lesson: for a spatial+pointwise filter, ISOLATE the spatial param to its
identity value (here Scan Line Brightness=1 => band factor 1) before reading a pointwise transfer;
a uniform-patch average silently folds the spatial modulation into the "transfer".
