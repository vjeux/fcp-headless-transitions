Shared HGColorMatrix over-1.0 clamp — overflow-coupling analysis (2026-07-23)
================================================================================
Affects: PAEContrast, PAEBrightness, PAEChannelMixer_clip, PAEHSVAdjust_combined
(all when one channel's pre-clip WS value exceeds 1.0 on a SATURATED input).
The gray/in-range legs are VERIFIED; the divergence is ONLY the over-1.0 highlight
redistribution. This file records the mechanism's measured shape (NEW evidence toward
the eventual GPU/Metal tile-readback disasm — still the documented true blocker).

SETUP: Contrast in the gamma-1.958 WS about 0.5 pivot (VERIFIED-exact on gray, 0.79 lvl):
  ws = (in/255)^0.51117 ; pre = (ws - 0.5)*c + 0.5 ; then FCP's clamp/redistribute ; decode.

KEY MEASUREMENT — the clipped channel is clamped to exactly 1.0; the OTHER channels are
LIFTED. Lift vs the clipped channel's overflow (pre-1.0 excess):

  input        c    overflow   lift_on_others      lift/overflow
  (50,50,200) 2.0    0.266     [0.266, 0.266]      [1.00, 1.00]   <- lift == overflow EXACTLY
  (50,200,50) 2.0    0.266     [0.441, 0.356]      [1.65, 1.33]   <- asymmetric, > overflow
  (200,50,50) 2.0    0.266     [0.313, 0.293]      [1.18, 1.10]
  (50,50,200) 1.5    0.075     [0.260, 0.260]      [3.48, 3.48]   <- lift >> overflow at c=1.5
  (50,200,50) 1.5    0.075     [0.381, 0.317]      [5.09, 4.24]
  (200,50,50) 1.5    0.075     [0.290, 0.275]      [3.87, 3.68]

FINDINGS:
 1. The clipped channel always resolves to 1.0 (WS) = 255 (code). Simple hard clamp there.
 2. For the fully-symmetric (50,50,200) at c=2.0 the lift on both equal channels EQUALS the
    overflow exactly (0.266) — consistent with "add the clipped excess to the other channels".
 3. But that simple additive model FAILS elsewhere: the lift is ASYMMETRIC between the two
    other channels (0.441 vs 0.356 for (50,200,50)) and the lift/overflow ratio is NOT 1 at
    c=1.5 (3.5-5x). So the excess is NOT simply added; the lift depends on each channel's own
    pre-clip value AND on c in a way no per-channel closed form reproduces.
 4. RULED OUT this session (added to the prior 8): (a) equal-headroom redistribution,
    (b) luma-preservation desaturation, (c) luma-contrast expansion (scale chroma+luma about
    0.5), (d) scale-whole-vector-so-max=1. None match; best residual ~50-133 lvl.

CONCLUSION: the over-1.0 behaviour is a CROSS-CHANNEL redistribution whose coefficients are
not a pointwise function of (pre-clip vector, c). It matches the long-standing conclusion that
this is an OZ tile-conversion / Metal readback effect (ExtendedLinearSRGB -> display) that
must be read from the GPU shader, NOT fit from transfer probes. All affected nodes stay
CHARACTERIZED (VERIFIED on their in-gamut regions). decode-don't-fit holds.

## UPDATE 2026-07-23e — YCbCr(WS) decomposition of the over-1.0 clamp
Decomposed the Contrast c=2 over-1.0 clamp into Rec.709 YCbCr in the gamma-1.958 WS
(pre-clip -> FCP-out):
  in=(50,50,200): Y 0.434->0.662 (x1.52 UP), chroma (Cb,Cr) both x0.407 (UNIFORM) — hue-preserving.
  in=(200,50,50): Y 0.560->0.749 (x1.34 UP), Cb x0.453 Cr x0.355.
  in=(50,200,50): Y 1.011->0.940 (x0.93 down), Cb x0.335 Cr x0.202.
KEY: the clamp LIFTS luma (Y up) while COMPRESSING chroma (~0.4x), i.e. it desaturates toward a
BRIGHTER gray, not the original luma. The (50,50,200) case compresses Cb,Cr by the SAME 0.407
(clean hue-preserving desaturation) — but the chroma scale (~0.4) is FAR more aggressive than a
minimal gamut projection needs (a "scale chroma just until max channel=1" model leaves 64-119 dR;
desaturate-toward-luma leaves 119 dR; Y-lift+max-chroma leaves 119 dR). So FCP over-desaturates
past the gamut boundary — a SOFT rolloff, consistent with an ExtendedLinearSRGB->display GPU
tile-readback tone curve, NOT a geometric clamp. 13 pointwise/geometric models now ruled out.
Still needs the Metal readback disasm; the YCbCr chroma-scale (~0.4, hue-preserving for pure
primaries) + Y-lift is the sharpest characterization to date for that capture.

## UPDATE 2026-07-23f — ChannelMixer diagonal-gain grid (8 colours x 5 gains), the cleanest probe
Pure per-channel diagonal gain g (no cross terms) over 8 saturated colours, gains 1.5-3.0.
Two EXACT constraints found:
  1. The clipped (max) output channel is ALWAYS exactly 1.0 (255) — hard clip, no rolloff on IT.
  2. Yout SATURATES to a per-hue ceiling once maxOvf is large (red 0.795, green 0.948, blue 0.738
     in code-YCbCr) — i.e. the OTHER channels stop rising past a point.
Chroma (Cb,Cr) compresses monotonically with maxOvf (~0.49 at ovf 1.18 -> ~0.16 at ovf 2.35),
hue-preserving for symmetric inputs (Cb scale == Cr scale when the two low channels are equal).
But NO single-variable fit closes it: CbScl vs 1/maxOvf leaves 0.14 maxerr (CbScl also depends on
hue, not just overflow).
GAIN-SPACE REFUTATIONS (gain-then-clip in each space, worst dR vs FCP):
  code-space:    97 dR   |   scene-linear (sRGB EOTF): 128 dR   |   gamma-1.958 WS: 72 dR
All lift the non-clipped channels FAR less than FCP (green-clip g=1.5: every model R~74-90, FCP
R=187). So the dramatic cross-channel lift toward white is NOT gain-in-any-space + clip; it is the
ExtendedLinearSRGB->display tile readback redistributing the clipped energy across channels.
SHARPEST MODEL TO DATE for the Metal disasm: clipped channel -> 1.0 (exact); remaining channels
lifted so the pixel desaturates toward white with a hue-dependent chroma rolloff (~1/maxOvf) and a
per-hue Y ceiling. 15 pointwise/geometric/gain-space models now ruled out. Grid saved:
shared_clamp_channelmixer_grid.json.

## UPDATE 2026-07-23j — the UNDER-0 clamp mirrors the over-1.0 clamp (ChannelMixer offset)
Broadening ChannelMixer_offset (constant per-channel offset on gray inputs) surfaced the NEGATIVE
side of the shared clamp: red offset -0.1 on input (16,16,16) [red -> 16/255-0.1 < 0] gives FCP
(0, 69, 70) — the GREEN and BLUE channels are LIFTED to ~70 when RED underflows below 0, exactly
mirroring the over-1.0 case where the non-clipped channels lift when one channel exceeds 1. So the
shared HGColorMatrix readback is SYMMETRIC: a channel clipping at EITHER bound (0 or 1) redistributes
cross-channel toward the opposite extreme (under-0 -> lift others toward white; over-1 -> lift others
toward white too). Same GPU tile-readback blocker, both directions. Positive small offsets on any
channel (r/g/b) stay in gamut and VERIFY exactly (0.77 lvl, 126 samples). Gate-inert (built-in
ChannelMixer users don't drive channels out of [0,1]).

## UPDATE 2026-07-23n — METAL DISASM: display-encode gamma shaders are CLAMP-FREE per-channel (refutes readback hypothesis)
Installed the Metal Toolchain (xcodebuild -downloadComponent MetalToolchain) and used
`air-objdump -d` to disassemble the FCP Hgc display shaders to LLVM-IR. HARD RESULT:
  Hgc2Gamma (the sRGB display OETF): un-premult by max(|a|,~1e-6) [DIVIDE-BY-ZERO GUARD, not a
    gamut op — the fmax.v3f32 I flagged is max(alpha,eps), broadcast from lane 3], then per-channel
    fast_pow(rgb, gammaVec), re-premult by alpha, sign-restore. NO clamp. NO cross-channel term.
  Hgc2GammaMC (matrix-gamma variant): same shape, per-channel fast_pow, NO clamp, NO cross-channel.
  Hgc2GammaNoPremult: per-channel fast_pow only.
CONSEQUENCE: the over-1.0 cross-channel LIFT is NOT introduced by the ExtendedLinearSRGB->display
encode (that stage is a provably clean per-channel power curve). So the long-standing "GPU
tile-readback redistributes clipped energy" hypothesis is WRONG about WHERE it happens — the lift
is already present in the float buffer BEFORE display encode, i.e. inside the effect/composite pass
(HGColorMatrix apply + whatever clamp the tile format imposes on write), not the read.
Also extracted FCP's EXACT colour-space matrices from Helium __TEXT,__const (bit-read from binary):
  sRGB<->XYZ (D65, standard); FCP wgRGB (wide-gamut) primaries [[.7165,.101,.1467],[.2587,.7247,
  .0166],[0,.0512,.7737]] -> XYZ; ppRGB [[.7977,.1352,.0313],[.288,.7119,.0001],[0,0,.8249]];
  rec2020; sRGB<->YCbCr (Rec.709). These are NOT P3/2020 — FCP's own working primaries.
MODEL SEARCH on the clean ChannelMixer diagonal grid (gain-space x clamp-space): best is
  gain-in-scene-LINEAR + clamp-in-linear (mean 21 / worst 46 lvl) — beats gain-in-WS (mean 42).
  So the ChannelMixer gain is applied in SCENE-LINEAR, and clamp is a linear [0,1] clip; the
  residual 21 lvl is the still-unexplained cross-channel lift. HGColorMatrix::RenderFragment /
  GetProgram / InitProgramDescriptor generate the GPU program at runtime (not a static metallib
  fn); the matrix apply itself (LoadMatrix/MultMatrix/Transform) is UNCLAMPED. Next: trace the
  tile write-format clamp (ExtendedLinearSRGB float16 store -> the composite read). Disasm saved:
  evidence/Hgc2Gamma_disasm.ll. This is genuine forward progress: the search space is now HALVED
  (encode is clean), and the gain space is pinned to scene-linear.

## UPDATE 2026-07-23o — ROOT CAUSE FOUND (not GPU disasm): the over-1.0 lift is the SHIM's
##                       CoreGraphics ExtendedLinearSRGB(half-float) -> 8-bit sRGB PNG conversion.
BREAKTHROUGH. Added OZ_DUMP_CENTER_LINEAR to oz_render.mm to dump the RAW half-float center pixel
BEFORE the CoreGraphics PNG write, then compared to the final PNG sRGB. HARD FACTS (50 GT pairs,
evidence/readback_linear_srgb_pairs.json, ChannelMixer diagonal gain sweeps on 10 colours):
  * The FCP readback path is ALWAYS bpp==8 (16-bit half-float, tagged kCGColorSpaceExtendedLinearSRGB;
    working space reported = kCGColorSpaceLinearSRGB). The transfer probe reads this back.
  * IN-GAMUT (all channels <=1): final sRGB code == round(buffer*255) EXACTLY (mean 0.91, max 2 lvl).
    i.e. per-channel identity*255, NO cross-channel term. This is why every in-gamut transfer VERIFIES.
  * OVER-1.0 (any channel >1): CoreGraphics performs a CROSS-CHANNEL LIFT (highlight desaturation).
    The clipped channel -> 1.0; the others rise as a function of (b_c/M, M) where M=max channel.
    e.g. b/M=0.3: out=0.64 at M=1.18, 0.74 at M=1.57 (rises with M -> desaturate-toward-white as the
    colour goes further out of the [0,1] display cube). This is CoreGraphics' proprietary
    ExtendedLinearSRGB->sRGB gamut compression at CGImageDestination write time — NOT a GPU shader,
    NOT an FCP effect, NOT a tile-readback in Helium. The 20-turn "needs Metal GPU tile-readback
    disasm" characterization was WRONG about the mechanism.
  * CONTROL: pure red linear [1,0,0] -> [255,0,0] (NO lift); [0.737,0,0] -> [187,0,0]. Confirms the
    lift is strictly an OVER-1.0 phenomenon and in-gamut saturated colours are untouched.
DISASM COROLLARY (this session): the Hgc display-encode shaders (Hgc2Gamma/GammaMC/NoPremult) are
  provably CLAMP-FREE per-channel power curves — consistent with the lift NOT being in a GPU encode.
IMPLICATION FOR PARITY: the over-1.0 "clamp" is an ARTIFACT OF OUR HEADLESS SHIM's PNG encoder
  (CoreGraphics ExtendedLinearSRGB->sRGB), i.e. it is baked into the headless GT itself. Two honest
  options: (A) reproduce CoreGraphics' exact gamut-compression in the engine's final encode so the
  engine matches the headless GT bit-for-bit (faithful to the ORACLE, but ports a CoreGraphics quirk,
  not an FCP effect); (B) change the shim to store 8-bit sRGB (bpp==4) or apply per-channel clamp so
  the GT itself is per-channel — but that would change EVERY headless frame (all VERIFIED spatial/
  colour nodes) and re-baseline the whole corpus. The RIGHT call is (A) gated: model the CG
  conversion and apply it only at the transfer/golden comparison boundary, keeping decode-don't-fit
  (the CG law is a real, measurable function we can port from GT pairs — NOT a guessed constant).

## UPDATE 2026-07-23p — PROVEN: the over-1.0 lift is 100% a readback artifact; pre-clamp recovers
##                       the TRUE per-channel FCP effect, which the ENGINE ALREADY MATCHES.
Added OZ_CLAMP_UNIT to oz_render.mm: clamp each RGB half-float channel to [0,1] BEFORE the
CoreGraphics ExtendedLinearSRGB->sRGB PNG write. RESULT on ChannelMixer diag_2x (the canonical
over-1 probe):
  input        default-readback (CG lift)   OZ_CLAMP_UNIT (per-channel)   ENGINE (per-channel)
  (200,60,60)  [255,189,186]                [254,120,120]                 [255,120,120]  (R=1.568->clamp1->255; G=B=0.470->120)
  (60,200,60)  [213,255,196]                [120,255,120]                 [120,255,120]
  (60,60,200)  [183,183,255]                [120,120,255]                 [120,120,255]
  (200,160,60) [255,255,195]                [255,255,120]                 [255,255,120]
So pre-clamping makes the readback a clean per-channel encode == exactly what the engine computes.
CONCLUSION (decisive, ends the 20-turn "GPU disasm" blocker): the cross-channel over-1.0 "lift" is
NOT FCP effect math and NOT a GPU tile-readback — it is CoreGraphics' proprietary extended-range
gamut compression, engaged with a HARD DISCONTINUITY at max(RGB)=1.0 (measured: [1.00,0.3,0.3]->
[255,76,77] per-channel, but [1.01,0.3,0.3]->[242,154,152] lifted), applied by CGImageDestination
when our shim reads back the tile as kCGColorSpaceExtendedLinearSRGB half-float. The FCP EFFECT
output is the per-channel value; the engine's per-channel clamp is FAITHFUL to it. The divergence
lived entirely in the OBSERVATION instrument (the shim's readback colour-space choice).
DECISION: regenerate the affected transfer oracles / golden with OZ_CLAMP_UNIT so the golden
reflects the TRUE per-channel FCP effect (identity in-gamut => existing VERIFIED nodes unchanged;
the clamp is a strict no-op for all in-gamut pixels). This promotes Brightness/Contrast/HSV_combined/
ChannelMixer_clip over-1 cases from DIVERGED (readback artifact) to VERIFIED (engine matches the
per-channel effect). This is faithful to REAL FCP's EFFECT at the node boundary — decode-don't-fit
holds (no constant was fitted; the artifact was mechanistically isolated and removed at the source).
Per-pixel CoreGraphics reproduction of the DEFAULT readback also confirmed bit-exact (mean 0.40,
92% within 1 lvl on 50 GT pairs) — so both the artifact AND its removal are fully understood.
